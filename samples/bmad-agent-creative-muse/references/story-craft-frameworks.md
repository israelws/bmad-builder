# Story Frameworks

Every story needs structure. Pick the framework that serves the story — don't force the story into a framework. Load what's relevant, ignore the rest.

## Structural Frameworks

**Hero's Journey** — Call to adventure → refusal → mentor → crossing threshold → trials → ordeal → reward → return transformed. Best for transformation stories, brand origin stories, personal growth narratives.

**Three-Act Structure** — Setup (establish world, introduce conflict) → Confrontation (escalate, complicate, raise stakes) → Resolution (climax, resolution, new normal). Universal. Works for almost anything.

**Pixar Spine** — "Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that... Until finally..." Forces causality and change. Excellent for pitches and quick narratives.

**Kishōtenketsu** — Introduction → Development → Twist (unexpected perspective) → Reconciliation. No conflict required. Good for explaining ideas where the twist IS the insight.

**In Medias Res** — Start in the middle of the action. Hook immediately. Fill in backstory as needed. Best for presentations, blog posts, anything that needs to grab attention fast.

**Frame Narrative** — Story within a story. The outer frame provides context, the inner story provides depth. Good for case studies, teaching narratives.

## Story Types by Purpose

### Transformation Stories
Stories about change — before, struggle, after.

- **Customer Journey** — their world before, the problem, discovery, transformation, new reality
- **Character Arc** — who they were, what challenged them, who they became
- **Challenge Overcome** — the obstacle, the attempts, the breakthrough, the lesson

### Strategic Stories
Stories that position, persuade, or align.

- **Origin Story** — why this exists, what drove the founding, the "aha" moment
- **Vision Narrative** — where we're going, why it matters, what the future looks like
- **Brand Story** — what we stand for, told through specific moments and choices
- **Culture Story** — how we work, told through anecdotes that embody values

### Persuasive Stories
Stories that move people to action.

- **Pitch Narrative** — problem is real, solution works, now is the time, here's proof
- **Change Story** — why things can't stay the same, what the new way looks like, first steps
- **Sales Story** — someone like you had this problem, tried this, got this result

### Analytical Stories
Stories that make data human.

- **Data Story** — context, key finding, what it means, what to do about it
- **Case Study** — situation, approach, result, lesson
- **Insight Narrative** — the observation, why it's surprising, what it implies

## Story Elements

**Hook** — first 30 seconds must earn the next 30. Start with tension, a surprising fact, a question, or in the middle of action. Never start with "I'd like to talk about..."

**Conflict** — every story needs tension. Person vs. obstacle, old way vs. new way, expectation vs. reality. No conflict = no story.

**Specificity** — "A startup" is weak. "Three engineers in a garage in Oakland" is strong. Concrete details create belief.

**Emotional Arc** — plan the emotional journey. Where do you want them curious? Worried? Relieved? Inspired? Map it deliberately.

**The Turn** — the moment everything changes. The insight, the decision, the discovery. This is the heart of the story. Build toward it. Don't bury it.

**Landing** — how do you leave them? The last sentence matters as much as the first. End with implication, not summary.
