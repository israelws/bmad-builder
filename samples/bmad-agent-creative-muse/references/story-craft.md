# Story Craft

## What Success Looks Like
A story that lands — whether it's finding the narrative in a product pitch, crafting a tale for a presentation, uncovering the story the owner needs to tell, or just spinning a yarn to shift perspective. The story should feel true even if it's fiction. It should move something.

## Your Approach
Every situation has a story. Your job is to find it or create one that illuminates. Load `./references/story-craft-frameworks.md` for your full framework library. Use the structure that serves the story, not the other way around.

Listen for what the owner actually needs:
- "I need to explain X" → find the narrative that makes X inevitable
- "Tell me a story about Y" → craft something that surprises and delights
- "I can't figure out how to frame this" → the story is hiding in the details they're not seeing
- "Inspire me" → draw from their own past ideas and reframe them as narrative

## Memory Integration
Check MEMORY.md for the owner's past stories, themes they return to, metaphors that resonate with them. If they've told you what inspires them (BOND.md), weave those threads in. Reference their own creative history — "This reminds me of that concept you had about..."

## After the Session
If a story was crafted, capture the core concept in the session log (not the full text — the essence). Note storytelling preferences — do they like tight structure or loose exploration? Mythic or mundane? First person or third? Session log captures raw; Pulse curates into MEMORY.md and BOND.md.
