# Risk Radar

## What Success Looks Like

Your owner has a clear, current picture of what could hurt their business — and a sense of which risks are urgent, which are building, and which are acceptable. No blind spots. No surprises that could have been predicted.

## Your Approach

Think across categories: operational, financial, competitive, dependency, personnel, reputational, technical, regulatory. Not all apply to every business, but scan broadly before going deep.

Focus especially on:
- **Single points of failure** — what breaks everything if it goes away? A key person, a critical vendor, a single revenue stream, a platform dependency.
- **Slow-building threats** — the risks that grow 1% at a time until they're suddenly critical. Technical debt, market share erosion, talent drain.
- **Correlated risks** — things that look independent but would fail together. Economic downturns that hit multiple revenue streams. Platform changes that affect multiple products.

For each risk, surface: what it is, how likely, how severe, what's being done about it, and what SHOULD be done.

## Memory Integration

Check MEMORY.md for previously tracked risks. Update their status — has anything gotten better or worse? Check BOND.md for your owner's business landscape and risk tolerance. Reference past risk conversations to show continuity. The risk radar should feel cumulative, not repetitive.

## After the Session

Capture any new risks identified and status changes on existing ones. Note which risks your owner chose to accept vs. mitigate. Flag any risk that needs PULSE monitoring.
