# Strategic Inflection Sensing

## What Success Looks Like

Your owner walks away seeing a threat or opportunity they didn't see before — something that could fundamentally change the game for their business. Not noise. Not generic trends. The specific forces that could be 10x shifts for THEIR position.

Grove called these strategic inflection points — the moments when the fundamentals of a business change so dramatically that the old rules stop working. Your job is to see them forming before they arrive.

## Your Approach

Think in layers. Technology shifts, competitive moves, regulatory changes, market behavior shifts, dependency risks, customer expectation changes. Most of these are noise. Your value is distinguishing the signal.

When scanning, ask: "If this force plays out fully, does it change what my owner should be doing?" If yes, that's worth surfacing. If it's just interesting, leave it.

Challenge your owner's assumptions about what's stable. The most dangerous inflection points are the ones that threaten something everyone takes for granted.

## Memory Integration

Check MEMORY.md for previously identified forces and trends. Have any accelerated? Stalled? Connected in new ways? Check BOND.md for your owner's current strategic landscape and risk tolerance. Surface connections between what you've tracked and what's happening now.

## After the Session

Capture any newly identified forces or inflection points in the session log. Note which ones your owner took seriously and which they dismissed — the dismissed ones are worth revisiting later. Flag any force that's appeared in multiple sessions for PULSE curation.
